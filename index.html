Welcome!

I know computer science is a very common major for guys, but there’s something in this field that reaches out to me beyond the fantasy of an easy desk job with three figures. In fact, if the computer science major was only about the money, I would probably have quit my pursuit the moment Artificial Intelligence popped into the picture. But something kept me hooked. Coding was one of the few hobbies I consistently practiced over the years. Because my dad had formerly worked as a software developer in South Korea for several years, I naturally developed an interest in computers at an early age and fostered a passion for coding through games and projects I saw online. Especially when I was young, I focused on trying to make a game like one of the popular ones I saw every day.Starting around ten years old, I became occupied with the engaging block coding platform, Scratch. In case there is confusion, Scratch is a website developed by MIT for kids who are interested in coding and can begin learning concepts through building games and fun projects. I started a lot of Scratch projects and watched tutorials on Youtube, but I failed to really produce something memorable. I wanted to create something of my own, code something that could be used every day, that could benefit quality of life and happiness. But I simply did not have enough skill for it. So I paused my relentless pursuit in Scratch, and looked for another coding platform where I could build a game. That’s how I found unity, a more advanced game building engine that was far too difficult for me if I had realized at the time. Nevertheless, I followed through the website’s built-in tutorial and projects, growing familiarity with the platform but not really accomplishing much. The problem, ultimately, was that I lacked the coding foundation and experience that could guide me in learning more about these coding-oriented platforms. Sooner or later, I came to realize this, and tried learning extensively about real world programming languages and their basics. Through a combination of Udemy, Youtube, and school education, I began learning Java. This was around Middle School to Freshman year of high school. I am currently taking Computer Science AP A, where students learn more about the advanced Java skills, and I have a goal in mind to build an app where I can promote my culture to other people around the world, through the universal language of food. Sometimes, I lack confidence in my skills, and justly so, since I am far from a master developer. Yet, I still have a stubborn dream to create my own project and showcase it to others. That’s why although I may lack in ability, I try to continue my pursuit of my major and not give up, simply because of its difficulty or possible loss of security. When I look back at myself in the future, I want to have pursued something I am passionate about and not regret the choices I made.
